Get Started

So now we know what calisthenic is, how do you get started with it? The introduction to calisthenics can be scary, especially if you look up videos on Youtube. The best way to get into calisthenics is to just start doing the basic exercises. You can introduce harder or cooler exercises when you get stronger and get more control of your body. You can find the 4 basic areas of calisthenics on this page, where all other exercises originate from. Build your program from these exercises and the linked videos.

Push-Up

Push-ups are one of the core exercises in every calisthenic program. It's a great exercise with hundreds of variations and different degrees of difficulty. Push-ups target mainly your chest but also activate your arms, shoulders, and core. Diffrent variations of the excersice targets diffrent areas more. The best thing about this exercise is that everybody can do it! If you cant does a standard push-up, go down on your knees and perform it. You can even elevate your hands to make it even easier.

Pros

  • Full Body Activation
  • Improved Posture
  • No Equipment Needed
  • Increased Cardiovascular Health
