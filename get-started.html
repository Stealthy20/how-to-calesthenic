Get Started

So, now that we know what a calisthenic is, how do we begin? Calisthenics can be intimidating at first, especially if you watch videos on YouTube. Starting with the fundamental movements is the greatest approach to begin into calisthenics. As you gain strength and control over your body, you can adopt harder or cooler activities. This page contains the four core regions of calisthenics, from which all additional exercises stem. These exercises, as well as the films connected to them, can be used to create your own program.

Push-Up

Every calisthenic program should include push-ups as one of the core activities. It's a fantastic exercise with a multitude of variations and levels of difficulty. Push-ups primarily work your chest, but they also target your arms, shoulders, and core. Different forms of the exercise concentrate on different areas. The best part about this activity is that everyone can perform it! If you can't do a regular push-up, go down on your knees and do one. You may make it even easier by elevating your hands. Pull Up

The king of back exercises is the pull-up. It can be done in a variety of ways with different grips to target different muscle groups. Even though the pull-up is the most difficult of the fundamentals, it may be changed to make it easier. Using resistance bands or an austrailian pull-up is an option. Abdominal

You can't ignore your abdominals if you want to continue progressing in your calisthenic adventure. It's not only important to keep your abdomnimals in good shape for your journey. It is even more important for your overall health. Proper posture and the relief of lower back pain require a strong core. Especially if you, like the majority of people, spend the majority of your day sitting. There are several muscle groups to work on. Squats

Squats are one of the few leg-strengthening exercises available. This is an area that is frequently overlooked in the realm of calisthenics. It's a crucial area to improve in order to continue to improve as an athlete while also preserving muscular balance in the body and avoiding injuries.
