Get Started

So, now that we know what a calisthenic is, how do we begin? Calisthenics can be intimidating at first, especially if you watch videos on YouTube. Starting with the fundamental movements is the greatest approach to begin into calisthenics. As you gain strength and control over your body, you can adopt harder or cooler activities. This page contains the four core regions of calisthenics, from which all additional exercises stem. These exercises, as well as the films connected to them, can be used to create your own program.

Push-Up

Every calisthenic program should include push-ups as one of the core activities. It's a fantastic exercise with a multitude of variations and levels of difficulty. Push-ups primarily work your chest, but they also target your arms, shoulders, and core. Different forms of the exercise concentrate on different areas. The best part about this activity is that everyone can perform it! If you can't do a regular push-up, go down on your knees and do one. You may make it even easier by elevating your hands.

Pros

  • Full Body Activation
  • Improved Posture
  • No Equipment Needed
  • Increased Cardiovascular Health
